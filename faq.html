Everything you need to know about donor recovery and hydration.

General Questions

What makes Vital Return different from sports drinks like Gatorade?

Sports drinks are designed to replace what you lose through sweat. Vital Return is scientifically formulated to replace what you lose through plasma donation. While sports drinks offer basic hydration, they lack the protein required to restore your serum levels and the specific vitamin blend needed to combat post-donation fatigue. We are the first all-in-one solution designed specifically for the donor’s physiology.

Is Vital Return only for plasma donors?

Definitely not. While we engineered Vital Return to solve the extreme depletion caused by plasma donation, that same high-performance formula makes it an incredible recovery tool for athletes. Whether you’ve just finished a donation or a heavy workout, your body is craving the same things: rapid rehydration and protein for muscle repair. Think of Vital Return as a sports recovery drink that has been "stress-tested" for medical-grade fatigue—if it works for a donor, it will work wonders for your post-gym recovery.

Usage & Timing

When is the best time to drink Vital Return?

For best results, drink Vital Return immediately (within 15 minutes) after your donation to jumpstart your recovery, stabilize your blood sugar, and replenish your fluids.

Pro Tip: You can also drink it 60–90 minutes before your appointment. The extra protein and hydration can help you pass your health screening and make the blood draw faster. Note: If drinking before, sip slowly to ensure you don’t spike your heart rate before your vitals check.

Can I drink this during my donation?

If your center allows water bottles on the donor floor, absolutely. Sipping Vital Return during your donation is a great way to stay ahead of dehydration and keep your calcium levels up.

How do I mix it?

Mix one packet (or scoop) with 16-20oz of cold water. Shake well for 20 seconds. Because we use high-quality whey isolate, it may foam slightly—this is normal! Let it settle for a minute for the perfect clear consistency.

Ingredients & Dietary

How much protein is in a serving?

17g of protein per serving. We use Whey Isolate because it absorbs quickly, helping replace the serum protein lost during the plasmapheresis process.

Does it contain caffeine?

No. We focus on natural energy through dextrose and hydration, ensuring you can go home and rest without disrupting your sleep schedule.

Does Vital Return help with the "tingles" (Citrate Reaction)?

Yes. The "tingling" sensation (or metallic taste) is caused by the anticoagulant used during donation, which temporarily lowers your calcium levels. Vital Return is fortified with Calcium to help buffer your body against this reaction, so you can skip the chalky antacids and just enjoy your drink.

Will this help my Iron / Hematocrit levels?

Vital Return is not an iron supplement, but it is a powerful ally for your iron levels. We include 500mg of Vitamin C (550% DV), which is clinically proven to significantly increase the absorption of iron from food.

Is this safe for diabetics?

Vital Return contains Dextrose, a fast-absorbing sugar designed to restore blood glucose levels quickly. If you are diabetic or monitoring your blood sugar, please consult your physician before using this product.

Is it dairy-free / vegan?

No. Vital Return uses Clear Whey Isolate, which is derived from milk. However, Whey Isolate is highly refined and contains very little lactose compared to standard Whey Concentrate, making it easier on the stomach.
